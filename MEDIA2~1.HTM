3   Recommendations

Vusela Risk Services is dedicated to delivering exceptional service by providing reliable, intelligence-driven offsite monitoring that supports your organisation's broader risk management objectives. Through a structured review of surveillance activity, incident trends, and system performance during the reporting period, we have identified strategic opportunities to strengthen security resilience and operational efficiency. The following recommendations are presented to enhance risk mitigation, optimise system effectiveness, and ensure the offsite CCTV solution continues to deliver measurable value.

Perimeter Camera Placement
To enhance early detection and proactive threat identification, it is recommended that CCTV cameras be installed directly along the perimeter fencing. Experience and operational data indicate that perimeter line camera placement delivers a significantly higher success rate in proactive monitoring and early intervention. In contrast, internal camera coverage primarily supports a reactive response once a breach has already occurred.
Virtual Guard Patrols and Advanced AI Monitoring
Vusela Risk Solutions operates an advanced AI-driven monitoring platform capable of applying up to 1,000 configurable detection rules. These rules generate alerts based on specific behaviours and actions, rather than people or vehicle detection alone. The Virtual Guard Patrol solution further enhances site oversight through randomised or scheduled virtual patrols, providing a comprehensive and timely view across all cameras. This approach strengthens situational awareness while reducing exposure and reliance on physical guarding resources.
Electric Fence Installation
The installation of an electric perimeter fence is strongly recommended as a deterrent measure. Electric fencing has a direct influence on criminal decision-making by increasing the perceived risk of detection and apprehension. This control significantly reduces the likelihood of unauthorised entry and minimises exposure to external threats before they reach the inner perimeter of the premises.
4   Key Crime Trends

Western Cape Business-Crime Overview

SAPS Q2 Report · Period: July 2025 – September 2025
In comparison with the SAPS Q2 2024 report, there has been a significant decrease of 26.8% in burglary incidents targeting business premises within the Western Cape. Truck hijackings also recorded a decrease; however, the reduction was limited to 6%, and this crime category remains a concern nationally. The Western Cape continues to contribute 2.9% of total truck hijacking incidents across South Africa. During the Q2 reporting period, a total of 1,632 bakkies and panel vans, as well as 58 trucks exceeding a 3,500 kg load capacity, were reported stolen. The Western Cape has further experienced a substantial decrease in business robberies of 28.1%, with the province accounting for 6.4% of all business robbery incidents nationally.
